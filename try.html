United Kingdom(England,Scotland,Wales,Nothern Ireland)

The United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland, commonly known as the United Kingdom (UK) or Britain,is a country in Western Europe, off the north-western coast of the continental mainland.It comprises England, Scotland, Wales and Northern Ireland. The United Kingdom includes the island of Great Britain, the north-eastern part of the island of Ireland, and many smaller islands within the British Isles.Northern Ireland shares a land border with the Republic of Ireland; otherwise, the United Kingdom is surrounded by the Atlantic Ocean, the North Sea, the English Channel, the Celtic Sea and the Irish Sea. The total area of the United Kingdom is 242,495 square kilometres (93,628 sq mi), with an estimated 2023 population of over 68 million people.

The coronation chair

The image on the right is the image of the coronation chair which was made in 1300s.The Coronation Chair, also known as St Edward's Chair or King Edward's Chair, is a wooden chair used by British monarchs to sit on when they are crowned at their coronations. It was made in 1300-03 by Walter of Durham and is 6.5 feet tall. The chair is made of Baltic oak and has been used for every coronation of an English monarch since 1308, with a few exceptions.King Edward I commissioned the chair in 1296 to house the Stone of Scone, which he had taken from the Scots in the 1200s. The chair is kept in Westminster Abbey in London, and during the coronation, it is placed in the center of the "Cosmati pavement" mosaic floor, facing the high altar, to emphasize the religious nature of the ceremony.

The chair has been used for every coronation of an English monarch since 1308, with a few exceptions. For example, a copy of the chair was made for the coronation of Queen Mary II in 1689.

Buckingham palace

Buckingham Palace is a royal residence in London and the administrative headquarters of the monarch of the United Kingdom.Located in the City of Westminster, the palace is often at the centre of state occasions and royal hospitality. It has been a focal point for the British people at times of national rejoicing and mourning.Originally known as Buckingham House, the building at the core of today's palace was a large townhouse built for the Duke of Buckingham in 1703 on a site that had been in private ownership for at least 150 years. It was acquired by King George III in 1761 as a private residence for Queen Charlotte and became known as The Queen's House. Buckingham Palace became the London residence of the British monarch on the accession of Queen Victoria in 1837.

The Diamond Jubilee State Coach

The Diamond Jubilee State Coach is a six-horse-drawn carriage built in Australia to commemorate Queen Elizabeth II's 80th birthday. The coach was originally called the State Coach Britannia, and was purchased by the Royal Collection Trust in 2012. It was completed in 2010, but didn't arrive in London until March 2014 due to funding issues. The coach was first used at the State Opening of Parliament on June 4, 2014, and has since been used to transport the sovereign.

The coach is black with gold details and a frieze around the top that features the national emblems of the United Kingdom. It's made predominantly of aluminum and carved from oak, and is drawn by six Windsor Grey Horses. The coach combines traditional craftsmanship and modern technology, with an aluminum body and six hydraulic stabilisers to prevent swaying.

The Gold State Coach

The Gold State Coach is a part of the British royal family's history and was used in the Platinum Jubilee Pageant in 2022. The coach is 29 feet long, 12 feet high, and weighs four tons. It has eight horses that draw it, and is decorated with gilded sculptures, painted panels, and carvings. The coach has been part of royal events since it was built in 1762, including Queen Elizabeth II's coronation in 1953, and King Charles III's coronation in 2023.The coach is made of giltwood, which is a thin layer of gold leaf over wood. The interior is lined with velvet and satin, and the coach's body is slung by braces covered with Morocco leather and decorated with gilt buckles. The coach also has painted panels by Giovanni Battista Cipriani, and elaborate carvings by Sir Joseph Wilton.The Gold State Coach is a dazzling, living part of British history. The iconic carriage which featured spectacularly at Queen Elizabeth II's Platinum Jubilee pageant and at the Coronation of King Charles III, is on display at the Royal Mews, the working stables of Buckingham Palace.
